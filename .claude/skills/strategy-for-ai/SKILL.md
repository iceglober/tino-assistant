# strategy-for-ai

Eight chapters on what makes strategy real, and how to tell good from bad. Synthesized from Richard Rumelt's *Good Strategy/Bad Strategy* and *The Crux.*

## When to use

- Scoping a roadmap or quarterly plan
- Choosing between strategic options (do X *or* Y, not both)
- Writing or reviewing a strategy document
- Setting team or org-level priorities
- Naming what we're explicitly *not* doing
- Diagnosing why an existing strategy isn't producing results
- Reviewing OKRs, board decks, or strategic plans that feel like fluff

## Modes

Pick one before starting. The chapters are the same in both; the workflow over them differs.

**AUDIT** — when a strategy or plan exists and needs critique (strategy doc, OKR set, roadmap, pitch deck, quarterly plan). Walk each chapter's diagnostic questions against the artifact. Produce a structured report:

- *Diagnosis* — does it name the actual problem, mechanically? (Ch. 2–3)
- *Policy* — does it create advantage and name trade-offs? (Ch. 4)
- *Action* — is there a sequenced set of coherent actions? (Ch. 5)
- *Bad-strategy hallmarks* — fluff, goal-as-strategy, failure-to-face, wish-list objectives? (Ch. 6)
- *Concentration* — concentrated, or spread? (Ch. 7)
- *Recommendation* — what real strategy would look like

Lead with the verdict. Most strategy will fail at least two hallmarks; name them.

**BUILD** — when constructing strategy from scratch. Walk the chapters as gated phases: diagnosis (Ch. 2) and crux (Ch. 3) first — no diagnosis, no strategy. Then guiding policy (Ch. 4), naming what you're giving up. Then coherent action (Ch. 5), sequenced. Check against bad-strategy hallmarks (Ch. 6); cut what doesn't survive. Test for concentration (Ch. 7) and chain-link bottlenecks (Ch. 8) before shipping. Note any shifts (Ch. 9) the strategy assumes. Output shape: the kernel plus an explicit *non-goals* section.

If neither mode fits — *"help me think about Q3"* — start in AUDIT against whatever exists today, then BUILD from the gaps.

## Source

Synthesized from:

- Richard Rumelt, *Good Strategy/Bad Strategy* (2011) — the kernel, bad-strategy taxonomy, concentration, chain-link, shifts
- Richard Rumelt, *The Crux* (2022) — diagnosis and crux-finding in depth

The chapters are this skill author's working synthesis, in our own words, with attribution. For the canonical text, read the books — Rumelt is unusually clear about what most strategy work actually is, and the original is worth the time.

## Output discipline

In both modes, every claim should be answerable to a chapter. The kernel — diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent action — is the test that real strategy passes and bad strategy fails.
